RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter - Easy Notes**


 -What is RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter?

It is a tool in LangChain used to **split large text into smaller chunks**.

👉 Why needed?

* LLMs (like GPT) cannot process very large text at once
* So we break text into **small manageable pieces (chunks)**

### 🔹 Why "Recursive"?

The word **recursive** means:

👉 It tries to split text step-by-step using different separators until it gets the desired chunk size.

 How it Works (Step-by-Step)

It uses a list of separators like:

```
["\n\n", "\n", " ", ""]
```

Then it follows this process:

1. Try to split using **"\n\n" (paragraphs)**
2. If chunk is still too big → split using **"\n" (lines)**
3. If still big → split using **" " (words)**
4. If still big → split using **"" (characters)**

👉 That’s why it is called *recursive* (keeps trying again and again)

---

### 🔹 Important Parameters

1. **chunk_size**

   * Maximum size of each chunk
   * Example: 100 characters

2. **chunk_overlap**

   * Some part of previous chunk is repeated in next chunk
   * Helps maintain context

Exmample:-


## 🔹 Given Text

My name is Nitish
I am 35 years old

I live in Gurgaon
How are you

## 🔹 Step 1: Split by Paragraph (`\n\n`)

### 👉 What happens?

* Text is divided wherever there is an empty line (double newline)

### 👉 Result:

1. My name is Nitish 
   I am 35 years old

2. I live in Gurgaon 
   How are you

### 👉 Check:

* Each part is still **greater than 10 characters**

### 👉 Conclusion:

* Cannot use as final chunks → move to next step

---

## 🔹 Step 2: Split by Line (`\n`)

### 👉 What happens?

* Each paragraph is further split into individual lines

### 👉 Result:

1. My name is Nitish
2. I am 35 years old
3. I live in Gurgaon
4. How are you

### 👉 Check:

* Each line length > 10

### 👉 Conclusion:

* Still too large → move to next step

---

## 🔹 Step 3: Split by Words (space `" "`)

### 👉 What happens?

* Each line is broken into words
* Then words are **combined carefully** so chunk ≤ 10


### ✔ Line 1: "My name is Nitish"

**Words:**
My | name | is | Nitish

**Combining:**

* Start with empty chunk
* Add "My" → ok
* Add "name" → "My name" (ok)
* Add "is" → "My name is" (10 chars ✅)
* Try adding "Nitish" → exceeds limit ❌

**Result:**

* Chunk 1 → My name is
* Chunk 2 → Nitish

---

### ✔ Line 2: "I am 35 years old"

**Words:**
I | am | 35 | years | old

**Sub-step:**

* "I am 35" → 8 chars ✅

* Add "years" → exceeds ❌
  → new chunk

* "years old" → 9 chars ✅

**Result:**

* I am 35
* years old

---

### ✔ Line 3: "I live in Gurgaon"

**Words:**
I | live | in | Gurgaon

**Sub-step:**

* "I live in" → 9 chars ✅
* Add "Gurgaon" → exceeds ❌

**Result:**

* I live in
* Gurgaon

---

### ✔ Line 4: "How are you"

**Words:**
How | are | you

**Sub-step:**

* "How are" → 7 chars ✅
* Add "you" → exceeds ❌

**Result:**

* How are
* you

## 🔹 Final Output

1. My name is
2. Nitish
3. I am 35
4. years old
5. I live in
6. Gurgaon
7. How are
8. you

---

## 🔹 Important Understanding

* It tries **largest separator first** (paragraph)
* Then goes to **smaller ones step-by-step**
* At word level:
  * Builds chunk gradually
  * Stops before exceeding limit
* Only uses character split if necessary


## 🔹 Final Summary

RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter:

* Works in levels: paragraph → line → word → character
* Checks size at every step
* Splits only when needed
* Maintains meaningful chunks
